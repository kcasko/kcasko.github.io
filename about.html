About Me :: Keith Casko

Welcome to TaurusTech. This is my workshop on the web.

I grew up in the era of dial-up modems, CRT monitors, and software that came with manuals thicker than a phone book. Those early years left a permanent mark. I gravitate toward systems that make sense, tools that do real work, and technology you can understand instead of blindly clicking through.

From Baker to Builder

For over twenty years, I worked in professional baking. That world runs on precision, timing, repetition, and problem solving under pressure. When one variable is off, everything downstream breaks.

Over time, it became clear that the skills I relied on every day were already technical. Optimizing workflows, diagnosing failures, and producing consistent results from complex systems. I was thinking like an engineer long before I changed the tools.

Building Systems, Not Just Code

I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering at Western Governors University. My focus goes beyond writing code. I care about how systems fit together from the hardware layer up through the operating system, networking, and application stack.

I learn by breaking things, fixing them, and rebuilding them better.

What I Work With

My work spans scripting, automation, and systems administration. That includes Python and Bash for automation, Windows Server environments with Active Directory and Group Policy, Linux systems such as Ubuntu and Kali, virtualization with VMware and VirtualBox, and modern development tooling like Git and VS Code.

This site documents that work. The wins, the failures, and everything in between.

The Home Lab

Most of my learning happens in a home lab where theory meets reality. I build and manage Windows Server domains from scratch, maintain virtualized and dual-boot environments, and write scripts to automate real problems.

It is my proving ground for troubleshooting, experimentation, and deep system understanding.

Long-Term Direction

While software engineering is my academic path, my long-term direction is cybersecurity. You cannot defend systems you do not understand. Everything I build now lays the groundwork for that path, from operating systems and networking to security-focused tooling.

TaurusTech exists to document that journey and to build useful things along the way.

If you are into systems, learning by doing, and understanding how things actually work under the hood, you are in the right place.
