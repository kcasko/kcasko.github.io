About Frankenmuth

Frankenmuth is a small town in Michigan known for its Bavarian heritage, festive atmosphere, and the famous Bavarian Inn. The city is a popular tourist destination, particularly during the holiday season.

Frankenmuth is often called Michigan's Little Bavaria, and it has a distinct German-inspired architecture and culture. Visitors can enjoy unique shopping, dining, and annual events like the Frankenmuth Oktoberfest.

Key Facts About Frankenmuth

  • Population: 5,235 (as of 2020 Census)
  • Year Incorporated: 1848
  • Region: Mid-Michigan (Saginaw Valley)
  • Classification: Rural/Suburban
  • Average Income Level: The average income in Frankenmuth is above the state average, largely due to its tourism-driven economy.
